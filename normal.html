Body Dysmorphia Disorder

Even those with a healthy weight can experience body dysmorphia, which is a mental health condition where a person becomes overly preoccupied with perceived flaws in their appearance. It can affect self-esteem and daily life.

Recommendation

  • Seek therapy or medications such as anti-depressants as needed.
  • Engage in mindfulness practices like yoga and deep breathing exercises.

Food Recommendations:

  • Focus on fatty acids and plant-based foods (e.g., fatty fish, walnuts, chia seeds, fruits, and vegetables).
  • Eat carbohydrates to provide energy and support overall body function.

Exercise Recommendations:

  • Moderate aerobic exercise (e.g., walking, cycling) to improve mood and reduce stress.
  • Include strength-building exercises to maintain overall health.
