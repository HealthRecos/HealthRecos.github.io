Normal Health Risks

If you are of normal weight, you may still be at risk for some health conditions, including Body Dysmorphia Disorder. It's essential to take care of your mental and physical well-being.

1. Body Dysmorphia Disorder

Body Dysmorphia Disorder (BDD) is a mental health condition where individuals spend excessive time worrying about perceived flaws in their appearance. These flaws are often unnoticeable to others. The condition can lead to distress, excessive mirror-checking, or avoiding social situations. It can significantly affect self-esteem and daily life.

Recommendation:

  • Consult a General Practitioner or a health specialist. Take therapies or medications (such as anti-depressants) if needed.
  • Engage in daily mindfulness practices such as guided meditation, yoga, and deep breathing.

Food Recommendation:

Incorporate fatty acids and plant-based foods for mental wellness and to reduce anxiety and depression. Foods like fatty fish, chia seeds, walnuts, fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can be beneficial.

Exercise Recommendation:

Moderate aerobic exercise such as walking, cycling, or swimming will improve mood, reduce stress, and alleviate anxiety and depression. Focus on overall health rather than body appearance.
