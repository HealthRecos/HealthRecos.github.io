Obesity and Its Health Risks

Obesity increases the risk of numerous health problems, including heart disease, stroke, diabetes, and more. Here are the key health risks and recommendations:

Health Risks:

  • Heart Disease (Coronary Artery Disease): Obesity is linked to high blood pressure and high cholesterol, increasing the risk of heart disease.
  • Type 2 Diabetes: Obesity is a major risk factor for insulin resistance and type 2 diabetes.
  • Sleep Apnea: Excess weight can contribute to breathing problems during sleep.

Recommendations

Food Recommendations:

  • Focus on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
  • Avoid sugary drinks, processed foods, and excess alcohol.

Exercise Recommendations:

  • Engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week.
  • Incorporate strength training exercises to improve muscle mass.
