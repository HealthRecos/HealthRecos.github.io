Obesity Health Risks

If you are obese, you may be at risk for the following health issues:

1. Cardiovascular Disease

A group of disorders of the heart and blood vessels, including coronary artery disease, which can lead to heart attack or stroke. Obesity increases blood pressure and cholesterol levels, both major risk factors.

Recommendation:

  • Aim to lose 5–10% of your body weight to significantly reduce risk.
  • Regularly monitor blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

Food Recommendation:

Eat heart-healthy foods such as whole grains, lean proteins, nuts, and leafy greens. Avoid trans fats, sugary drinks, and excessive sodium.

Exercise Recommendation:

Engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week, such as brisk walking or swimming. Incorporate strength training exercises 2–3 times a week.

2. Type 2 Diabetes

A chronic condition where the body becomes resistant to insulin or doesn't produce enough, leading to high blood sugar levels. Obesity is a major risk factor due to its impact on insulin sensitivity.

Recommendation:

  • Maintain a calorie-controlled, balanced diet and monitor blood sugar levels regularly.
  • Gradually increase physical activity to improve insulin sensitivity.

Food Recommendation:

Focus on low-glycemic foods such as vegetables, legumes, and whole grains. Limit refined carbs, sugary snacks, and processed foods.

Exercise Recommendation:

Engage in a mix of aerobic activities like cycling or walking and resistance training to manage blood sugar. Incorporate high-intensity interval training (HIIT) for additional benefits.
