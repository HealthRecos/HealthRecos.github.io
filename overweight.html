Overweight Health Risks

If you are overweight, you may be at risk for the following health issues:

1. Cardiovascular Disease

Overweight individuals are at an increased risk of cardiovascular diseases, including coronary artery disease. The additional weight puts extra strain on the heart and increases blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

Recommendation:

  • Aim for gradual weight loss to reduce risk factors.
  • Regularly monitor your blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

Food Recommendation:

Eat heart-healthy foods such as vegetables, fruits, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Avoid processed foods, excessive sugar, and salty snacks.

Exercise Recommendation:

Incorporate 150 minutes of aerobic activity per week, such as brisk walking, swimming, or cycling. Include strength training exercises twice a week.

2. Type 2 Diabetes

Overweight individuals are more likely to develop type 2 diabetes due to decreased insulin sensitivity. Weight loss and exercise can improve blood sugar control.

Recommendation:

  • Maintain a balanced diet with low glycemic foods to manage blood sugar.
  • Gradually increase physical activity to improve insulin sensitivity.

Food Recommendation:

Focus on foods like whole grains, legumes, non-starchy vegetables, and lean proteins. Avoid sugary snacks, refined carbs, and processed foods.

Exercise Recommendation:

Incorporate both aerobic exercises and resistance training. Consider high-intensity interval training (HIIT) to improve metabolic health.
