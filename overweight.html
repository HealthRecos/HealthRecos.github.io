Overweight and Its Health Risks

Being overweight comes with increased risks of health problems such as cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and joint issues. Here's what you should know:

Health Risks:

  • Heart Disease: Overweight individuals are more likely to have high blood pressure, which can contribute to heart disease.
  • Type 2 Diabetes: Excess weight affects insulin sensitivity, increasing the risk of developing diabetes.
  • Joint Problems: Overweight individuals are at risk for joint pain, arthritis, and back problems.

Recommendations

Food Recommendations:

  • Eat nutrient-dense foods like fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
  • Limit refined carbs and processed foods to manage weight effectively.

Exercise Recommendations:

  • Engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week.
  • Incorporate strength training and flexibility exercises.
